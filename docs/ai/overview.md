The integration of AI within QED is currently exploratory, with a focus on augmenting—not replacing—human insight. 
At this stage, our primary objective is to leverage AI for script generation based on user stories. This involves training the system on the full set of available page objects within QED, enabling it to:

- Understand and utilize all exposed properties and functions of each page object.
- Identify gaps in coverage and suggest the creation of new page objects where necessary.
- Translate user intent into executable, idiomatic test scripts with minimal friction.

A secondary—but equally promising—application lies in report interpretation. Here, AI could assist in:

- Categorizing and contextualizing test failures.
- Distinguishing between genuine defects and environmental or transient issues.
- Proposing likely causes and potential resolutions based on historical patterns and telemetry.
- These capabilities are still in development, and their effectiveness will evolve as QED’s object model and telemetry grow more expressive. Our goal is not automation for its own sake, but intelligent augmentation—making test authoring and analysis faster, clearer, and more resilient.
